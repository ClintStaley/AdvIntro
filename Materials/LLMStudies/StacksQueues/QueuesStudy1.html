Queues

Comparator

The priority queue implementations all make use of a Comparator to define the meaning of "less" and "greater" for whatever objects are being added to the priority queue. One cannot use < or > for other than elemental numerical types, so some means of determining order is necessary. Discuss Comparator with Claude. Have Claude grade a specific quiz for this one, asking you to write, unassisted, four Comparator classes for LinkPQueue<String> that prioritize by increasing lexicographic order, decreasing order, case-insensitive increasing order, and length of string.

Comparable

Related but subtly different from the Comparator interface is the Comparable interface, for types that provide their own built-in comparison rule. String is an example, but there are others, which you should find out.
