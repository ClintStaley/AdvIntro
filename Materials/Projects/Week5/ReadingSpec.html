Queues

We will have time to cover Stacks in class, but the three Queue implementations: LinkQueue, DblLinkQueue, and ArrQueue, are your job to understand, in detail. Do this by attending the optional Tuesday session this week if you like, and either way, with LLM assistance. Be sure that you understand, and are ready to be tested on:

1. General doubly-linked list operations, including how a head node simplifies the code, addition and removal of nodes, etc.

2. Why we can't just test ArrQueue for emptiness using only the values of mHead and mTail, and need mEmpty.

3. Why we have special case code for empty queue in LinkQueue.

4. Why Object can be used to stand for any type

5. What the <> notation is about.

6. How an "Iterator" works

But note, that's just a short list, not comprehensive. Your grade on this reading assignment will depend on your interaction with the LLM. I will not even supply a list of questions to ask it; that's your job. I'll look for the following elements in your conversation:

1. Deeper questions about the concepts than just an overview of the code.

2. Extending on and/or improving the code.

3. Some sort of generated quiz from the LLM, to test your comprehension, especially of pointer/reference operations.

4. Catching the LLM in an error.
